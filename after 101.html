1. Introduction & Overview

Purpose

This guide is your complete reference for handling After Rental cases at Europcar UK. Every process, decision tree, and template you need is contained within these pages.

What is an After Rental Case?

After Rental cases are customer contacts received after the vehicle has been returned and the rental agreement has been closed. These typically involve:

  • Invoice disputes
  • Charge queries
  • Refund requests
  • Document requests
  • Service complaints
  • Damage/fines queries

Service Level Agreements (SLAs)

  • Case resolution: 5 working days
  • UK RFI response: 2 working days
  • International RFI response: 3 working days
  • Authorization requests: 2 working days

Key Principles

  1. Every case deserves a substantive response - never just search offers or disclaimers
  2. Evidence-based decisions - use systems, documentation, and data
  3. Customer empathy - acknowledge their experience while presenting facts
  4. Revenue protection - maintain charges when documentation supports them
  5. Accuracy over speed - take time to investigate thoroughly

4. Investigation Framework

Step-by-Step Investigation Process

Step 1: Understand the Customer's Issue

Read the email thoroughly and identify:

  • What is the customer disputing/requesting?
  • What specific charges are in question?
  • What dates/rental agreement are referenced?
  • Has the customer provided any evidence?
  • What outcome does the customer expect?

Step 2: Complete GDPR Check

Before accessing any customer data, verify identity:

For emails:

  • Verify email matches customer account
  • If mismatch: Send Data Protection email
  • Wait for response from verified email before proceeding

Never provide information to third parties without authorization

Step 3: Gather Evidence from Systems

Greenway Investigation:

  1. Reservation Screen (6,1): Confirm booking details, rates, inclusions
  2. Invoice Enquiry: Review all invoices, check F12+E on charges, F7 for notes (CRITICAL!)
  3. Helpdesk Screen (Screen 17): Verify vehicle details, payment history, notes section
  4. Vehicle Movement Screen: Track vehicle by registration

Documentation Check (Priority Order):

  1. Salesforce "Retrieve All Docs": Check first
  2. KGS: UK rentals (5 working day wait)
  3. D&C App: Delivery/collection photos and paperwork
  4. FileNet: International corporate countries

Required Documents for Different Disputes:

Dispute Type Required Documentation
Charges Signed RA + AOP (if applicable)
Fuel RA + SOR/Check-in docs + D&C photos + Connexus data
Days Charged RA + SOR + Connexus geofence data
Equipment RA + AOP + D&C photos
Coverage RA + AOP (both must be 100% correct)
Damage Riskappli documents (24hr wait)
Fines SAFO documents

Step 4: Check for Notes and Communications

  • Greenway F7 Notes (Invoice screen): ALWAYS check before RFI
  • Salesforce Case History: Previous contacts on same issue
  • D&C App Notes: Delivery driver observations

Step 5: Cross-Reference Terms & Conditions

Key T&C Sections:

  • Section 5: Vehicle usage
  • Section 16: Cancellations
  • Section 20: Fuel policy
  • Section 21: Data usage
  • Section 23: Liability limitations

Step 6: Identify Root Cause

Ask yourself:

  • Who is responsible? (Customer, station, system, standard process)
  • What evidence supports this?
  • Is documentation sufficient to maintain?

Step 7: Determine If RFI Required

Send RFI When:
  • Customer claims station told them something different
  • Documentation missing (after 5 working days for UK)
  • Need station's version of events
  • Charge applied but no supporting evidence visible
DO NOT Send RFI For:
  • Charges clearly visible on signed RA/AOP
  • Connexus data answers the question
  • Documentation available on systems
  • Terms & Conditions question
  • Deposit authorization queries

5. Decision Making Guidelines

The Decision Framework

Every After Rental case requires you to make ONE of these decisions:

  1. Maintain the charge (Outcome: Charges Upheld)
  2. Refund the charge (Outcome: Refund)
  3. Partial refund (Outcome: Refund)
  4. Provide explanation only (Outcome: Explanation)
  5. Offer gesture of goodwill (Outcome: Apology/Upgrade Coupon)

Maintain Charge When:

‚úÖ Documentation Complete and Correct

  • Signed RA + AOP present (if applicable)
  • All fields completed accurately
  • Amounts match charges applied
  • Customer signature clear
  • 100% correct - no errors allowed

‚úÖ System Data Supports Charge

  • Connexus shows fuel missing/mileage accurate
  • SixPay confirms transaction
  • Greenway notes explain charge
  • D&C photos support invoice

‚úÖ Terms & Conditions Support Charge

  • Charge explicitly covered in T&C
  • Tariff Guide lists fee
  • Mandatory charge per country rules
  • Customer agreed to T&C at booking

Refund Charge When:

‚ùå Documentation Missing or Incorrect

  • No signed RA after 5 working days (UK)
  • AOP not signed
  • Amounts on AOP don't match invoice
  • Fields left blank or incorrect
  • Documents illegible

‚ùå System Data Contradicts Charge

  • Connexus shows vehicle returned full
  • D&C photos show equipment was missing at pickup
  • Mileage telemetry shows charge impossible
  • Payment already refunded per SixPay

‚ùå Evidence of Station Error

  • F7 notes confirm mistake
  • RFI confirms charge incorrect
  • Duplicate charge visible
  • Wrong rate applied vs signed RA

Charge-Specific Decision Trees

Fuel Charges

Maintain If Refund If
  • Connexus shows fuel missing at return
  • D&C collection photos show not full
  • SOR/Check-in docs show level < full
  • Customer admits not refueling
  • Over 60 miles driven + gauge below full
  • Connexus shows returned full
  • D&C photos show full tank
  • Customer receipt shows refuel before return
  • Under 60 miles + no documentation
  • Delivery showed missing fuel

Days Charged / Grace Period

Grace Periods:
  • Standard customers: 29 minutes
  • Star customers: 2 hours (UK only)

Coverage Charges (CDW/THW/Protection Packages)

Maintain If Refund If
  • RA AND AOP both signed and 100% correct
  • Amounts on AOP match invoice
  • Product on original booking
  • Mandatory coverage per country T&C
  • AOP missing or not signed
  • Amounts incorrect on AOP
  • Not on booking AND not on paperwork
  • US/Canadian customer with card coverage proof

Deposit Not Released

Explain Only (No Refund Due):
  • Authorization only (not debit) - released 3-10 working days by bank
  • SixPay reversal receipt sent - customer to contact bank
  • Partial release (some used for charges)
  • Within 30-day authorization period

Refund Amount Calculations

Full Refund:

  • Entire charge in question
  • No documentation to support ANY of it
  • Station/system error affecting whole rental

Partial Refund:

  • Part of charge justified, part not
  • Pro-rata calculation needed
  • Example: 3 days charged, 2 days justified = refund 1 day

VAT Considerations:

  • Greenway refunds include VAT automatically
  • Allowances: remove VAT first (divide by 1.20 for UK)
  • Example: ¬£60 refund = ¬£50 allowance (60/1.2)

When to Escalate/Seek Guidance

Mandatory Escalation:

  • Refund exceeds your authorization limit
  • Customer threatens legal action
  • Fraud suspected
  • GDPR breach identified
  • Managing Director mention
  • Media involvement

Authorization Thresholds (UK Ops Staff):

Amount Required Authorization
¬£50 or under Station Manager
¬£51-¬£150 Territory Manager
¬£151-¬£2,999 Regional Director
¬£3,000+ Operations Director

3. Case Comments

Detailed internal notes explaining:

  • What was investigated (systems checked)
  • What was found (evidence discovered)
  • Decision made (maintain/refund/explain)
  • Action taken (refund processed, email sent, escalated)
  • Reason for decision (documentation support, lack of evidence, T&C, etc.)

Good Case Comment Example:

Customer disputes fuel charge of ¬£45.60 for 19 litres.

Investigation:
- Checked Connexus: Vehicle returned with 3/8 tank (62% full)
- Reviewed D&C collection photos: Fuel gauge visible showing not full
- SOR confirms check-in fuel level
- RA states full-to-full policy agreed

Decision: MAINTAIN CHARGE
Charge justified by Connexus data and D&C photos. Customer charged for actual litres missing (19L) at ¬£2.40/L as per signed RA.

Outcome: Charges Upheld - Connexus Justified
Root Cause: Customer
Email sent with Connexus PDF attached, explained full-to-full policy.

Bad Case Comment Example (Don't do this):

Fuel charge maintained. Sent email.

10. Specialized Scenarios & Edge Cases

Tour Operator Specific Procedures

RentalCars Bookings

Identification:

  • Booking source: Often LONC75, other city codes + C75
  • Account name: RentalCars, Travel Jigsaw
  • Method of payment: VCH (voucher)
Key Differences:
  1. Split Billing: RentalCars pays basic rental, customer pays extras
  2. GDPR: No Data Protection email needed (they are our customer)
  3. Voucher Coverage: Check F12+W for what's included
  4. Unused Days: Cannot refund unless medical/mechanical proof

Privilege/Star Customer Benefits

  • Free additional driver (varies by tier)
  • Grace period: 2 hours (UK only)
  • Priority service
  • Upgrade possibilities

International Hire Complexities

Greenway vs Non-Greenway Countries

Greenway Countries Non-Greenway Countries
  • UK, France, Germany, Spain, Italy, Belgium, Portugal, Netherlands
  • Full system visibility
  • FileNet may have documents
  • Refund via Greenway if UK has money
  • All others (Australia, NZ, Austria, Switzerland, etc.)
  • Reservation visible only
  • Must RFI for any information
  • Country decides on refunds
  • Use country chargeback forms

Vulnerable Customer Handling

Indicators of Vulnerability:
  • Mental health mentions
  • Bereavement during/affecting rental
  • Medical conditions impacting rental
  • Elderly with comprehension issues
  • Financial hardship expressed
Adjusted Approach:
  1. Extra patience: Allow time for understanding
  2. Simpler language: Avoid jargon, short sentences
  3. Repeat key points: Confirm understanding
  4. Empathy: Acknowledge their situation genuinely
  5. Flexibility: Consider policy exceptions where reasonable
  6. Documentation: Note vulnerability in case comments
  7. Escalation: If welfare concerns, escalate to TL

  2. Note what's making you uncertain
  3. Ask Team Leader BEFORE responding
  4. Never guess - better to ask

Customer Threatens Immediate Action (Legal/Media/MD):

  1. Remain calm and professional
  2. Complete investigation thoroughly
  3. Document everything in detail
  4. Escalate to Team Leader immediately
  5. Don't commit to resolutions without approval
